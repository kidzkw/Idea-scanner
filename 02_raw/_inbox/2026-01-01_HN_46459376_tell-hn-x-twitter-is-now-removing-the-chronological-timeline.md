# Tell HN: X/Twitter is now removing the chronological timeline

> **Source**: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46459376

## 原始内容

I really feel like the platform is actively fighting against my own agency, and I want to know if others are seeing this new behavior.<p>I am an engineer from Brazil working with Deep Learning and NLP. For years, my strategy to avoid social media addiction was simple: I curated a list of researchers and labs in my field, and I only browsed the &quot;Following&quot; tab. I ignore the &quot;For You&quot; tab completely. I treat it like an RSS feed: I want to see what happened in order, and when I reach the post I saw yesterday, I stop.<p>Some time ago (I think last year?), they split the timeline into two tabs: &quot;For You&quot; and &quot;Following&quot;. It was annoying that they removed the old global setting to default to chronological, but I could live with just clicking &quot;Following&quot; every time I opened the app.<p>But things got much worse last week.<p>My &quot;Following&quot; feed started feeling weird. Less informative, more controversial, and out of order. Then I noticed that inside the &quot;Following&quot; tab, there is now a dropdown menu to sort by &quot;Popular&quot; vs &quot;Recent&quot;.<p>It seems the default is now &quot;Popular&quot; (algorithmic). Even if I manually switch it to &quot;Recent&quot; to get my chronological order back, it resets on the next access.<p>They are effectively forcing the algorithm down our throats. I imagine an algorithmic timeline is much better for ad injection and &quot;doomscrolling&quot; engagement because every refresh gives you something new, whereas a chronological feed has a &quot;end&quot; state.<p>It is super frustrating. I really want to move away from X, but the signal-to-noise ratio for niche technical content is still too high there to just leave.<p>I just want to vent my frustration.
