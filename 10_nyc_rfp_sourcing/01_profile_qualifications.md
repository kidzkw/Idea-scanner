## 1. Minority group eligibility

| Group | Origin | Asian male qualifies? |
|---|---|---|
| Black | Sub-Saharan Africa | — |
| Hispanic | Mexico, Central/South America, Caribbean Spanish-speaking | — |
| **Asian-Pacific** | Far East, Southeast Asia, Pacific Islands (China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam, Philippines, Thailand, etc.) | **✅** |
| **Asian-Indian Subcontinent** | India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan | **✅** |
| Native American | — | — |

The user identifies as **Asian male sourcing from China** → fits **Asian-Pacific** category.

## 2. Ownership & control test (the part most applicants fail)

The minority owner must hold **at least 51% ownership** *and* exercise **operational
control**. SBS reviewers scrutinize:

- [ ] 51% of equity in name of the minority individual (not a holding entity)
- [ ] Capital contribution proportional to ownership (no "phantom" capital)
- [ ] Minority owner is highest-paid officer (or has documented reason otherwise)
- [ ] Minority owner signs banking documents, contracts, leases
- [ ] Minority owner makes day-to-day operational decisions
- [ ] No non-minority "silent partner" with effective veto rights
- [ ] No non-minority spouse community-property complications (NY is not CP state, but document anyway)

**Red flag pattern**: applicant fronts as 51% owner for a non-minority operator who
actually runs the business ("rent-a-minority"). SBS rejects these on interview.

---

## 3. Business eligibility

- [ ] Entity formed in NY *or* registered as foreign entity doing business in NY
- [ ] Physical NY business address (PO box not sufficient; coworking with mail-handling can work, varies by reviewer)
- [ ] In business **at least 1 year** (some categories require longer)
- [ ] **Independent** — not a subsidiary or affiliate of a non-MBE
- [ ] Meets SBA size standards for primary NAICS code (varies; for "All Other Misc Nondurable Goods Merchant Wholesalers" NAICS 424990 = 250 employees; for "Cut and Sew Apparel" 315240 = 750 employees)
- [ ] Personal net worth of minority owner under cap (NYC: **$3.5M PNW cap** excluding primary residence and the business itself, as of 2023 update; verify current)

---

## 4. Individual eligibility

- [ ] **US citizen or Lawful Permanent Resident** (green card)
- [ ] At least one of: birth certificate / passport / naturalization cert / I-551 showing eligible ancestry
- [ ] If naturalized: documentation tracing ancestry to qualifying region (rarely an issue for Asian-Pacific applicants with Asian birth records)

> **Visa holders** (H-1B, L-1, F-1 OPT, E-2, EB-5 pending) are NOT eligible until LPR.
> Confirm immigration status before proceeding. This is the most common disqualifier
> for Asian-American founders who built businesses while on work visas.

## 5. Stacking other certifications (do this in parallel)

| Cert | Issuer | Adds | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NYC M/WBE** | SBS | $1.5M direct-buy from NYC agencies | High (90–120 day review) |
| **NYS MWBE** | ESD | NYS agency contracts (MTA, CUNY, state DOT) | High — separate application |
| **Port Authority MBE** | PANYNJ OBD&CR | PANYNJ contracts (airports, bridges, World Trade) | Medium |
| **SBA 8(a)** | Federal SBA | Federal sole-source up to $4.5M (services) / $7M (mfg) | Very high (9-year program, social/economic disadvantage test) |
| **SDB / WOSB / VOSB** | Federal SBA | Federal small-biz set-asides | Lower — self-cert in some cases |
| **NMSDC certification** | National Minority Supplier Development Council | Fortune 500 supplier-diversity programs | Medium |

**Recommended sequence for this user**: NYC M/WBE first (highest leverage on stated
thesis) → NYS MWBE in parallel month 2 → PANYNJ + NMSDC in month 6.

## Decision

Before continuing to `02_procurement_landscape.md`, the user should be able to
answer **yes** to all of:

1. I am Asian-Pacific or Asian-Indian descent (verifiable on birth/passport docs).
2. I am a US citizen or LPR.
3. I either have, or am willing to form within 30 days, a 51%+ owned NY entity.
4. My personal net worth (ex-house, ex-business) is under $3.5M.
5. I can self-fund or borrow ~$50K working capital for first PO.

If any answer is **no**, the gate fails — re-scope the project before proceeding.
