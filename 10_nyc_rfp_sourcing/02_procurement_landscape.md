# NYC Procurement Landscape — Who Buys, How Much, How

> Map of NYC agencies → annual spend → buy channels → MWBE goods opportunities.

---

## The 4 channels (ranked by leverage for a new MBE)

### Channel A — MWBE Noncompetitive Small Purchase (THE PRIMARY TARGET)

- **Threshold**: up to **$1,500,000 per transaction** (raised from $500K → $1.5M in PPB rule amendment Jan 2024)
- **Mechanism**: agency Contracting Officer directly purchases from a City-certified MWBE; no public solicitation needed
- **Visibility**: agency-specific MWBE rolodexes. The vendor has to *be on the list*, not "respond to an RFP"
- **Cycle time**: weeks, not months
- **Win driver**: relationship + capability statement + responsive quote turnaround
- **Where to register**: PASSPort + each agency's MWBE liaison

### Channel B — M/WBE Set-Aside RFPs (PASSPort)

- Competitive but **restricted to certified MWBEs only**
- Larger ticket ($100K – $10M+)
- Posted publicly on [PASSPort Public](https://a0333-passportpublic.nyc.gov/rfx.html)
- Cycle time: 60–120 days from notice to award

### Channel C — Open Public RFPs (PASSPort + City Record)

- Full competition; MWBE is a tie-breaker or evaluation-weight bonus, not a gate
- Lowest win rate for a new entrant against incumbents like W.B. Mason, Grainger, etc.
- Useful for *reading the market* — see what categories actually get bought, at what price

### Channel D — Subcontracting to Primes

- Primes (large GCs, big distributors) have **MWBE subcontracting goals** (e.g. 30% on city contracts)
- They actively look for MWBE subs to count toward the goal
- Lower margin but lower customer acquisition cost
- Targets: turner, skanska, gilbane (construction), w.b. mason, office depot (goods distribution)

## Top agencies by addressable goods spend

| Agency | What they buy a lot of | Typical PO size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DCAS** (Citywide Admin Services) | Office supplies, furniture, vehicles, fuel, IT hardware, janitorial | $10K – $5M | Central procurement; many requirements contracts |
| **DOE** (Education) | School supplies, furniture, uniforms, sports equipment, electronics, PPE | $5K – $50M | Biggest single buyer; school-level micro-purchases also exist |
| **NYPD** | Uniforms, body armor, equipment, batteries, electronics, vehicle accessories | $10K – $10M+ | Has dedicated MWBE small-purchase page; very active |
| **FDNY** | Uniforms, PPE/turnout gear, hose, hardware, medical supplies (EMS) | $10K – $5M | Strict certifications (NFPA) — barrier to entry |
| **DSNY** (Sanitation) | Uniforms, gloves, bins, brooms, hardware, signage, salt | $10K – $20M | Huge consumables volume |
| **DOC** (Correction) | Uniforms (inmate + staff), bedding, food service, hardware | $10K – $5M | Politically sensitive — extra scrutiny |
| **Parks** | Lumber, hardware, signage, playground parts, plants, uniforms | $5K – $2M | Many small purchases |
| **DOT** (Transportation) | Signs, signals, traffic cones, paint, hardware, lighting | $10K – $20M | Heavy infrastructure component triggers Buy American on federal-funded |
| **DDC** (Design & Construction) | Construction materials (passes through to GCs) | Project-based | Subcontracting opportunity via primes |
| **NYCHA** | Apartment hardware, paint, locks, appliances, uniforms, janitorial | $10K – $20M | Federal HUD funding → BABA / federal Buy American rules apply |
| **HHC / NYC Health+Hospitals** | Medical supplies, PPE, linens, furniture | $10K – $50M | FDA + Joint Commission reqs on medical |
| **MTA** (state authority, not city) | Uniforms, hardware, signage, electronics, station supplies | $10K – $100M+ | NYS MWBE cert (not NYC); has DBE program for FTA-funded |
| **Port Authority** (bi-state) | Uniforms, signage, airport supplies | $10K – $50M | PANYNJ MBE program (separate) |

**Heuristic**: for a new MBE going after Channel A direct buys, prioritize agencies with
high consumables turnover and lower certification barriers — **DSNY, DCAS, NYPD,
Parks, DOC**. Defer FDNY (NFPA gear) and HHC (FDA medical) until reputation built.

## Buyer contact patterns

Every NYC agency has at least one **MWBE Liaison** or **Agency Chief Contracting
Officer (ACCO)**. The standing playbook:

1. Pull MWBE liaison email from the agency's "Doing Business With Us" page
2. Send a 1-page **Capability Statement** (PDF) attached + 3-line cover email
3. Follow up in 2 weeks; offer to drop off a physical sample if applicable
4. Ask: "What's coming up in the next 6 months in [category]? Who's the buyer?"

Known starting points:
- NYPD: `mwbe@nypd.org`
- DCAS: see [NYC.gov/DCAS](https://www.nyc.gov/site/dcas/business/dcas-agency-procurement-faq.page)
- DOE: see [NYC.gov/schools/about/financial/contracts](https://www.nyc.gov/site/schools/about/contracts.page)
- SBS Certification Unit (general): `mwbe@sbs.nyc.gov`

## Adjacent buyers (treat as bonus, not core)

- **CUNY** — books, lab supplies, furniture, IT (NYS MWBE)
- **HHC affiliated hospitals** — federated procurement, harder to crack
- **State DOT** — signage, hardware (NYS MWBE / DBE)
- **NYC Council member offices** — small discretionary funds, not RFP-driven but visible to MWBEs
- **BIDs (Business Improvement Districts)** — uniforms, signage, holiday decor; not city procurement but procurement-adjacent
